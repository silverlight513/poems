My name

My name may mislead you.
For as I stand right here,
I dare even put the strings of letters
Than is London before derry.
A fight I have no game in.
But an enemy as is mine,
Long and old we fight.
A war they now refute,
So short is their memory
For their misdeeds.

Reason/Description:
I normally try and steer away from political writings in my poetry but this one came to me. I'm not even sure it can be called a poem and more of just a mad ramble. I'd recently seen a few news articles by multiple different newspapers that told of struggles of non english people in the british isles. One that I was reminded of as I was going to sleep was of a woman who complained that because she has a Wrecsam accent, she gets mistaken for english all the time (Riveting news, I know). As I have also been mistaken for english and my name is very english. The facts that hold true to me don't bother me but I do protest being anything other than a Welshman. Anyway, I was in a poetic mood and this all just popped into my head. It hasn't been altered or worked on, it's read as it was thought then wrote.
