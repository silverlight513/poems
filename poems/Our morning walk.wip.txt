Working title: Our morning walk

1.
Along the northern path,
in morning sunny dew
We walked along the grassy track
Passing hazel, hawthorn and honeysuckle. 

2.
The sun shone bright on our backs.
That bright warm light that's so 
seldom found in early spring.
A newly welcomed warmth.

3.
Then I stood still for a moment,
taking a pause to just breathe.
A moment in time that's just mine.
My view, my breathe and my thoughts.

4.
And in this stillness, I feel a wash of calm.
An emotion that's been lost to me for so long
An overwhelming feeling of bliss
now that the noise has stopped.

5.
While I wait, I wonder;
How long will this new state last?
Will I succumb back to the mayhem
or continue on this new path?

6.
Looking back and forward along the track.
Left and right, through the bushes and trees.
Alone, I appreciate the moment,
observing the harmony around me.

7.
Over to our south, almost sunken out of view,
stood this large empty quarry,
laden with scattered rabbits,
digging and scouting the world around them.

8.
... Rabbits eager for life and new starts

9.
Then past the rocky depression,
We see the southern sea.
Uncharacteristically calm and settled.
Open and empty, void of sailors.

10.
... Normally the passing ships, so focused on their goal, no calm, no time, a schedule to keep.

11.
Before us, nothing dare move,
bar from windfull effects
of the waves and branches,
all up to the grass before my feet.

12.
And so the moment passes,
and we continue to walk,
along this often trodden path
around the corner into what may become.



... Can this stanza be added anywhere?
Sloe and hawthown bushes, 
that appear shrunk, peer over
from the abandoned rocky dugout
crawling up to the path.

Reason/Description:
I've recently had a bad foot and not gone on the usual walk that goes the length of the village between the coast and the train line. It's started getting better, as did the weather and figured I'd go for a morning walk with the dog. I've been wanting to do longer walks, as I find them usefull for keeping my mind from racing and fighting stress. When I reached the point where the path goes from being in a field to being surrounded by plants, I took a moment, realised my mind was calm for the first time in a while and I stood there to write the start of this poem.
