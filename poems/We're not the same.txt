We're not the same

When I see a tall wall of text
It overwhelms and frightens me.
I don't know if it scares you too,
Dear reader, I don't know you.
I don't know what you like 
I don't know what you dislike.
Maybe I'm unable to relate to you,
Unable to figure out what
Makes you feel and action.
But what I do know,
Is that you feel.
You have felt what I've felt.
Maybe for different reasons or the same
But at some point in our lives,
We have lived the same moment,
The same feeling with the same power
And for that I'm grateful
That I'm not alone.

Reason/Description:
I've been thinking about trying to get my poems out to the world more and in turn what poems would resonate with people the most. Over the past 6 months, I've noticed the more vague poems tend to do better, they tend to have more reception and more relation from the readers. While thinking about how people relate to what I write, I came up with this. It fascinates me that every person experiences and feels differently based off the same material and I think this might be the fascination that opens me up to writing more relatable work.
