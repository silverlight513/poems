Crying in a field

At the end of the day.
On sunset slewn grass,
with that special shade of green;
That's bolder than it should.

The wind dared blow,
inconsistent and strong
And I walked along the path
towards the cliffs edge.

It's sudden fall
with only prickly hedges.
The barrier between now and never;
I came to a stop.

Then one by one they ran,
Down my rosy cheeks
these tears streak.
Like streams and rivers flow.

Then the pain followed.
Uncomfortable and awkward
where the reason untold.
I'm frozen still, looking
and waiting for it to go.

Trees and grass alike
flail and thrash
in protest to the gale.
In contrast to my suspended
petrified self so pale.

Was it the wind,
irritating my eyes
Or the now relaxed mind
realising, way too late
what has always been my fate?

Has this pain always been here,
and if so, how long?
A secret hidden by myself or
thoughts ignored before.
Oh how I cry now, here and still.¡
